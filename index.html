How do physical breathing trainers improve lung capacity?

Physical breathing trainers utilize Inspiratory Muscle Training (IMT) to provide resistive loading to the diaphragm and intercostal muscles. Scientific research indicates that this "resistance training for the lungs" leads to muscle hypertrophy, improved neural recruitment patterns, and delayed onset of the respiratory muscle metaboreflex. In 2026 studies, users have shown up to a 28% increase in maximum inspiratory pressure and a significant enhancement in Forced Vital Capacity (FVC), allowing for deeper, more efficient oxygen delivery during exertion.
